Common Tourist Mistake: Many travellers assume Singapore requires formal clothing and complicated transport passes. In reality, a contactless credit card, Google Maps, and casual clothing cover most situations.

Before You Fly: Singapore Arrival Card (SGAC)

Before entering Singapore, travellers must complete the Singapore Arrival Card, an electronic immigration declaration required by the ICA. This form replaces the old paper arrival card.

  • Submit within 3 days before arriving.
  • Includes passport, flight, accommodation, and health declaration.
  • Takes 2–3 minutes and is completely free.

📌 Official Site: ica.gov.sg/sgarrivalcard

Important Warning: Many travellers accidentally submit information to third-party websites that charge $20–$50. Always use the official government site.
Local Insight: You can submit the SG Arrival Card while waiting at the airport before boarding your flight.

SIM Cards & Mobile Data

Mobile coverage is excellent and data speeds are among the fastest in Asia.

  • Airport counters sell $30–$50 tourist SIMs, but cheaper options exist.
  • The $12 Singtel hi!Tourist SIM includes 100GB of data (usually requires pre-booking online).
  • eSIM plans allow activation before arriving.
  • Local SIMs are recommended over global eSIMs to save phone battery.
Realistic Data Usage: Heavy users consume around 20GB per week. With 100GB, you never need to worry about limits or searching for public WiFi.

Power Plugs & Charging

  • Singapore uses the Type G power plug (same as the UK).
  • Electricity runs at 230V.
  • Most modern electronics support 110–240V; you only need a plug adapter.

Money & Payments

Singapore is one of the most cashless societies in the world.

  • Contactless credit cards, Apple Pay, and Google Pay are accepted almost everywhere.
  • Public transport uses the SimplyGo system (no separate card needed).
  • Carry small amounts of cash for hawker centres.
Typical Hawker Centre Prices: A typical meal usually costs around $8 to $12 SGD.

Getting Around: SimplyGo Transit

Tap your contactless Visa/Mastercard or mobile wallet at the MRT gate or bus reader. No need to buy tickets or transport cards.

  • Administrative Fee: Foreign cards incur a $0.60 SGD daily fee.
  • Individual Cards: Each traveller must tap their own card/phone.
  • Children: Under 7 years old and below 0.9m tall travel free.
  • Escalator Etiquette: Stand on the left. Walk on the right.

Taxis & Ride Apps

  • Taxi Lights: Green (Available), Red (Busy).
  • Changing Shift: Around 4PM–5PM, drivers may only take passengers heading in their direction.
  • Ride-Hailing: Grab is the most widely used platform.
  • Strict Child Seat Laws: Taxis are exempt, but private hire cars (Grab) are not. Use GrabFamily for vehicles with child seats.

Hawker Centres & Food Culture

  • Chope System: Use a packet of tissues or umbrella to reserve a table while ordering.
  • Mandatory Tray Return: You must return trays to racks to avoid fines (up to $300).
  • Halal vs Non-Halal Racks: Keep trays separate to respect Islamic dietary laws.

☕ Order Coffee Like a Local:

  • Kopi: Coffee with condensed milk
  • Kopi C: Coffee with evaporated milk
  • Kopi O: Black coffee with sugar
  • Peng: Iced | Siew Dai: Less sugar | Kosong: No sugar

Communication & Culture

Communication is direct and efficient. Skip long greetings at busy stalls.

  • Address older people as "Uncle" or "Auntie" as a sign of respect.
  • Singlish: Common words include Can (Yes), Cannot (No), Lah (Emphasis), and Liao (Finished).
  • Queueing: Extremely important. Never cut the line.

Laws Visitors Should Know

  • MRT: No eating or drinking ($500 fine).
  • Vaping: Completely illegal ($10,000 fine/arrest).
  • Alcohol: No shop sales or public drinking after 10:30 PM.
  • Jaywalking: Fines issued for crossing within 50m of a pedestrian crossing.
  • Durian: Banned on public transport and in many hotels due to smell.

✨ Insider Knowledge & Pro Tips

Discover Like a Local

Want to experience Singapore like a true local? Here are pro tips that go beyond typical tourist advice. Learn the hidden gems, best times to visit, and insider hacks that will elevate your entire trip.

Hidden Gems & Secret Spots

Lesser-Known Parks

Discover serene gardens and parks beyond the main tourist circuits for peaceful moments.

Local Bars & Lounges

Experience authentic nightlife spots favored by Singaporeans, not just tourists.

Instagram-Worthy Spots

Capture stunning photos at locations locals use instead of overcrowded tourist hotspots.

Best Food Experiences

Try authentic cuisines at holes-in-the-wall where locals actually eat daily.

Best Time to Visit

  • Dry Season (Feb-Apr, Jul-Sep): Best weather with less rainfall. Expect slightly cooler temperatures.
  • Monsoon Season (May-Jun, Oct-Jan): Afternoon showers are common. Fewer tourists, better deals on hotels.
  • Peak Holiday (Dec-Jan): School holidays = crowded attractions but vibrant festive atmosphere.
  • Shoulder Season (Feb-Mar, Aug-Sep): Ideal balance of good weather and manageable crowds.
  • Pro Tip: Visit major attractions on weekday mornings to avoid massive crowds.

Authentic Local Experiences

  • Join a Kopitiam Social: Locals gather at kopitiam (coffee shops) early morning. It's the real Singapore.
  • Visit Fish Markets: Bukit Merah or Kranji markets at dawn for authentic local energy.
  • MRT During Peak Hours: Experience the organized chaos of Singapore's commuters - it's uniquely Singapore!
  • Explore HDB Neighborhoods: Areas like Tiong Bahru, Bukit Timah, and Katong offer real local charm.
  • Join Meetup Groups: Connect with locals through meetup.com for cultural exchange and tips.
  • Attend Weekend Markets: Kampong Glam, Tiong Bahru, and neighborhood markets on weekends.

Money-Saving Pro Hacks

  • Hawker Food is Ridiculously Cheap: $3-5 for a full meal. Skip expensive restaurants for the best local experience.
  • Free Attractions: Marina Bay area, Singapore Botanic Gardens free entry, park walks, and riverside strolls.
  • Wise App for Transfers: Save significantly on forex fees compared to credit card/bank transfers.
  • Book Off-Peak MRT Travel: Use the contactless card system without tourist markup.
  • Hotel Rates Drop Mid-Week: Book Tue-Thu for 30-40% savings compared to weekend rates.
  • Use Grab Pools: Share rides to cut taxi costs in half during peak hours.
  • Mix Hawkers with Fine Dining: Have local food daily, splurge on 1-2 special meals.

Getting Around Like a Pro

  • MRT vs Grab: For short distances (<1km), MRT is cheaper. For longer routes at off-peak, Grab can be comparable.
  • Download Offline Maps: Save Google Maps areas offline for areas with poor signal.
  • Bus Routes Are Underrated: Often faster than MRT during off-peak and offer better sightseeing.
  • Walking Connections: Many MRT stations are within walking distance. Saves a few dollars daily.
  • Bike-Sharing: Mobike, oBike for short distances - only costs a few dollars per ride.
  • Tourist Day Pass: Consider a DayPass ($13 SGD) if doing 5+ transport trips in one day.

Culinary Adventures

  • Michelin-Awarded Hawker Stalls: Singapore has the most affordable Michelin stars globally - under $3 per meal!
  • Peranakan Cuisine: Try authentic Nyonya dishes at smaller restaurants in Joo Chiat and Katong.
  • Chendol vs Ice Cream: Traditional chendol (shaved ice) costs $1-2 vs $5+ for Western ice cream - equally satisfying!
  • Timing for Food: Visit hawkers 11am-1pm or 5pm-7pm for peak freshness and bustle.
  • Learn Food Order Codes: Ask vendors for recommendations. They know what's good and what's been sitting.
  • Craft Beer Scene: Singapore's craft beer bars offer local brews - much cheaper than Western brands.

Photography Pro Tips

  • Golden Hour Spots: Marina Bay Sands at sunset, Singapore River Cruise, Gardens by the Bay at dusk.
  • Avoid Mid-Day Sun: Best light is 6am-9am and 4pm-7pm for outdoor photography.
  • Unique Angles: Shoot from above at MRT bridges, from ground level at street markets, and from water boats.
  • Storm Photography: Tropical storms create dramatic skies - capture them safely from shelter.
  • People Photography: Ask permission at hawker centres. Locals often love sharing their stories.
  • Night Markets: Kampong Glam at night, Tiong Bahru with LED lights, offer vibrant street photography.

Rookie Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Don't Tip in Singapore: Tipping is NOT expected and can be seen as offensive in some contexts.
  • ❌ Don't Ignore Queue Culture: Queue-cutting will get you stared at and called out.
  • ❌ Don't Touch Street Vendor's Setup: Ask before moving items or taking photos. Respect their space.
  • ❌ Don't Wear Shoes Indoors at Temples: Remove footwear at religious sites. Show respect.
  • ❌ Don't Assume Prices: Some hawker stalls are cash-only or have minimum card purchases.
  • ❌ Don't Leave Possessions Unattended: Singapore is safe, but petty theft happens. Watch your bag.
  • ❌ Don't Skip Sunscreen: Equatorial sun is intense. Apply SPF 50+ and reapply frequently.

Final Pro Tips for the Perfect Trip

  • 🌟 Embrace the Heat: Slow down, have more frequent meals, drink more water. Singapore pace is different from Western rush.
  • 🌟 Join Locals for Food: Best conversations happen over hawker meals. Don't eat alone.
  • 🌟 Public Transport Etiquette: Offer seats to elderly/pregnant people. It's culturally important.
  • 🌟 Visit During Festivals: Chinese New Year, Deepavali, Hari Raya offer vibrant cultural experiences.
  • 🌟 Explore Beyond Marina Bay: Neighborhoods like Joo Chiat, Tiong Bahru, and Bukit Timah have more character.
  • 🌟 Build Relationships: Locals love helping genuine travelers. Chat with vendors, ask questions, be curious.
